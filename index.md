## About Me


![Profile Picture]({{ "assets/images/photo watermark.png" | relative_url }}){: class="profile-image"}

 
My name is Jingbo Wang, and I am an aspiring mechanical engineer in Cornell University. I like to build, design, and create! I come from the Bronx High School of Science. My work focuses on building a solid foundation in physics-based modeling, differential equations, and linear algebra, and applying those tools to real-world engineering problems. I enjoy breaking down complex systems, whether mechanical or computational, into clear, solvable components and then turning theory into practical implementation. Beyond coursework, I’m interested in research-driven projects that blend hardware, simulation, and optimization, and I’m continuously looking to expand my skills in technical problem-solving, system design, and analytical thinking.

Beyond coursework, I’ve pursued hands-on, design-focused experiences that align with my interests in aerospace and complex mechanical systems. Through the Space System Design Studio (SSDS), I’ve explored how propulsion, structures, guidance, and mission constraints integrate into a cohesive spacecraft architecture, strengthening my ability to think at the systems level rather than just the component level. Additionally, my involvement with Magnus Ultralight has deepened my understanding of lightweight aircraft design and performance trade-offs, exposing me to real-world considerations such as structural efficiency, aerodynamics, and weight optimization. Together, these experiences have sharpened my ability to approach engineering challenges holistically—balancing theory, constraints, and practical implementation.
