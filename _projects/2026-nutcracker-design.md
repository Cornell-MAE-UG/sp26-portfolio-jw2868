As part of a class assignment, I was tasked with designing a nutcracker.

This is how I solved the problem:

Find: I have to find a design a nutcracker, the usabability, and ultimately a modified version using a linear actuator

Given: For my assumptions I assumed that the nut laid rest on length L1, while L2 was where the distributed force of the hand ultimately is. Along with design assumptions, I made assumptions to a human's max grips strength which was 103lb, average nut diameter of 1 inch, and a force required to crack a nut of 490 lb. I assumed that the length of L1 to be 1.5 inches.

Now that I made these assumptions, I began to diagram my nutcracker and drew a free body diagram which showed the lengths, members, and location of forces. I then decided to 'explode' the members of the nutcracker. I solved for the net moment at point of the pivot. I was able to solve for the force of the output nutcracker in terms of the force applied and the ratio of the lengths. With this, I was able to solve for the length at which force has to be applied from, which was at least 7.14 inches in order to reach the minimum force necessary to crack the nut.

In terms of usability, the nutcracker is very efficient as it as a mechanical advantage of 4.76. However, the length of the nutcracker may be an issue depending on what L1 or L2 is as it decides the length of each other to maintain the length ratio.
